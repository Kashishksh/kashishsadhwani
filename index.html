  • City: Mumbai, India
  • Email: kashishksh@gmail.com

Kashish Sadhwani is a researcher specializing in the impacts of climate and land-use changes on hydrological extremes such as floods and droughts. With a Ph.D. from the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Bombay, his work combines advanced hydrological modeling, machine learning, and GIS techniques to assess and predict water-related risks in the face of climate variability. His research spans multiple domains, including flood-prone area detection, drought hazard assessment, and the study of compound hydrological events. Dr. Sadhwani has expertise in using models like the Variable Infiltration Capacity (VIC) and Soil and Water Assessment Tool (SWAT) for simulating hydrological processes, with a focus on improving flood forecasting, risk management, and resilience strategies. Throughout his career, Dr. Sadhwani has utilized high-resolution climate data, satellite imagery, and statistical methods to develop tools that aid in environmental management and policymaking. His work aims to bridge the gap between scientific research and practical applications, helping communities better adapt to the challenges posed by climate change and extreme weather events.
